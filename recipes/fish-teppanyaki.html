Fish Teppanyaki Recipe

Description

Fish Teppanyaki is a classic Japanese dish that features a beautifully grilled piece of fish, usually served with an assortment of vegetables. The fish is cooked on a teppan, a flat iron griddle, which gives it a distinctive taste and texture. The dish is often garnished with sesame seeds and a light soy-based sauce, enhancing its flavors and making it a delight for the senses. This meal is perfect for those who appreciate the art of Japanese cooking and the subtle interplay of flavors and textures.

Ingredients

  • 1 fillet of your preferred fish (e.g., salmon or seabass)
  • 1 tablespoon of soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of mirin
  • 1 teaspoon of sesame oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon of grated ginger
  • Assorted vegetables (e.g., bell peppers, zucchini, onions, and mushrooms)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Sesame seeds for garnish

Steps

  1. Preheat the teppan (iron griddle) over medium-high heat.
  2. Season the fish fillet with salt and pepper.
  3. In a small bowl, mix together soy sauce, mirin, sesame oil, and grated ginger.
  4. Lightly oil the teppan, then place the fish fillet and vegetables on the griddle.
  5. Cook the fish for 3-4 minutes on each side, or until it is cooked through and has a nice sear.
  6. Halfway through cooking, brush the fish and vegetables with the soy sauce mixture.
  7. Once cooked, transfer the fish and vegetables to a plate.
  8. Garnish the fish with sesame seeds and drizzle with any remaining soy sauce mixture.
  9. Serve hot and enjoy your homemade Fish Teppanyaki.
